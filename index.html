Operation Saheli

In galaxies where silence surrounds topics like menstrual health, we became the voice no one else dared to be. Operation Saheli — "Saheli" meaning friend in Hindi — wasn’t just a mission. It was a movement. We ventured into rural orphanages in India, where these conversations were nonexistent. With compassion in one hand and courage in the other, we created a space for 200+ girls to learn, speak, and be heard. We didn’t arrive as instructors — we arrived as Sahelis, friends, sisters, allies. With every diagram drawn and question answered, we rewrote the story of silence into one of strength. Because dignity is a right, not a privilege. And in that moment, we didn’t just teach biology — we shared humanity.
